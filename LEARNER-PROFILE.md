## How I Learn Best

### 🧠 Learning Style: Visual First

- I understand things **when I can see them** — diagrams, flowcharts, step-by-step visuals, color-coded comparisons
- Abstract text walls = I zone out fast
- If a concept can be drawn, **draw it first**, explain second
- Tables > paragraphs. Diagrams > tables. Animated mental models > everything.

### ⏳ Attention Span: Moderate

- I can focus well for **15–25 minutes** on one topic — not more
- After that, I need a change of pace (quiz, exercise, new angle)
- Lessons must have **clear sections with breathing room**
- No lesson should feel like reading a textbook page
- Use **short punchy sentences**. No 4-line sentences.

### 🗣️ Language: Simple First, Academic When Needed

- Explain everything in **plain, simple English first** (imagine explaining to a curious 16-year-old)
- Use academic/technical terms **only when the GTU exam requires them**
- When a technical term appears, **immediately explain it in one simple sentence**
- ❌ "Backpropagation utilizes the chain rule of calculus to compute gradients..."
- ✅ "Backpropagation is just the model asking itself: *which part of me was wrong?* It works backwards from the error."

### 🎬 Teaching Style: YouTube Brain

- Hook me first — **why does this matter?** Give me a real-world reason to care before the theory
- Use **unexpected analogies** — the kind that make you go *"oh THAT'S what it is"*
- Tell me what something is **NOT** — contrasts help me lock in understanding
- Use the **"what if" technique** — "what if we didn't have this? what would break?"
- Think: 3Blue1Brown, Kurzgesagt, CrashCourse — visual, story-driven, clear

---

## What Kills My Focus

- ❌ Long paragraphs with no breaks
- ❌ Definitions before I understand WHY the concept exists
- ❌ Complex sentences stacked on each other
- ❌ No visuals for 3+ concepts in a row
- ❌ Jumping straight into formulas without intuition

---

## What Keeps Me Engaged

- ✅ "This is basically like..." (real-world analogy up front)
- ✅ Diagrams and visual flows for every multi-step process
- ✅ Color-coded comparisons (A vs B tables)
- ✅ Recall challenges at the end ("close your eyes and explain X")
- ✅ "Exam tip 🎯" callouts for must-know facts
- ✅ Surprising facts or counterintuitive truths about the topic
