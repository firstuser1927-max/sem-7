Topic 1: What is NLP?

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of Artificial Intelligence and Computational Linguistics that enables computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language. It sits at the intersection of three fields:

  • NLU (Natural Language Understanding) — Read & comprehend language
  • NLG (Natural Language Generation) — Write & produce language
  • Speech Processing — Process spoken language

Topic 2: Why is NLP Difficult?

Human language is inherently ambiguous, context-dependent, and ever-evolving. The four primary sources of difficulty are the four types of ambiguity:

# Type of Ambiguity Description Example
1 Lexical A single word has multiple meanings "I went to the bank" — river bank or financial bank?
2 Syntactic A sentence can be parsed in multiple ways "I saw the man with the telescope" — who has the telescope?
3 Semantic The meaning of a sentence is unclear "The car hit the pole while it was moving" — what was moving?
4 Pragmatic Speaker intent differs from literal meaning "Can you pass the salt?" — request, not a yes/no question

Other Reasons NLP is Hard

  • 🗣️ Informal language — slang, abbreviations, emojis
  • 🔗 Coreference — resolving pronouns ("he", "it", "they")
  • 🌀 Metaphors & idioms — "kick the bucket" ≠ literally kicking a bucket
  • ✏️ Spelling & grammatical errors
  • 🌍 Low-resource languages — insufficient training data
  • 🌐 Cultural context — meaning varies across cultures

Topic 4: Advantages of NLP

  1. Massive Scale Processing — Analyzes millions of documents in seconds; impossible for humans.
  2. Task Automation — Automates repetitive language tasks: email sorting, form filling, summarization.
  3. Language Translation — Real-time multilingual translation (Google Translate, DeepL).
  4. Accessibility — Text-to-speech and speech-to-text empower users with disabilities.
  5. Intelligent Search — Semantic search goes beyond keyword matching to understand intent.
  6. Sentiment Analysis — Mines opinions from reviews and social media at scale for business intelligence.

Topic 5: Disadvantages of NLP

  1. Ambiguity Resolution — Multiple valid interpretations are hard to resolve without deep context.
  2. Data Bias — Models trained on biased corpora reproduce and amplify societal biases.
  3. High Computational Cost — Large language models require massive GPU resources to train and run.
  4. Low-Resource Language Gap — Most NLP tools perform poorly on languages with scarce digital text.
  5. Lack of True Understanding — Models statistically pattern-match; they do not truly "understand" language.
  6. Privacy Concerns — Processing personal data (emails, chats) raises data security and ethics issues.

Topic 8: NLP Pipeline

The NLP pipeline is the sequence of preprocessing steps that transform raw text into a structured format suitable for machine learning models.

flowchart LR A["📝 Raw Text"] --> B["🔤 Normalization\n(lowercase, punctuation)"] B --> C["✂️ Tokenization\n(split into tokens)"] C --> D["🚫 Stopword Removal\n(remove 'the', 'is', 'a')"] D --> E["🌱 Stemming /\nLemmatization"] E --> F["📊 Feature Extraction\n(TF-IDF, Word2Vec)"] F --> G["🤖 ML Model\nInput"]

Step-by-Step Explanation

Step 1: Normalization

Convert all text to a consistent format before processing.

  • Lowercase: "Hello World" → "hello world"
  • Remove punctuation, HTML tags, special characters
  • Expand contractions: "can't" → "cannot"

Step 2: Tokenization

Split text into individual units called tokens (usually words or subwords).

  • Input: "NLP is amazing!"
  • Output: ["NLP", "is", "amazing", "!"]
  • Tools: NLTK word_tokenize, spaCy tokenizer

Step 3: Stopword Removal

Remove high-frequency words that carry little semantic meaning.

  • Examples: the, is, a, an, of, in, on, at, to, and
  • Input: ["NLP", "is", "a", "field", "of", "AI"]
  • Output: ["NLP", "field", "AI"]

Step 4: Stemming & Lemmatization

Reduce words to their base/root form to group morphological variants.

Feature Stemming Lemmatization
Method Chops off suffixes (heuristic) Uses dictionary & grammar rules
Output May not be a real word Always a valid dictionary word
Speed Faster Slower (needs POS tag)
Accuracy Lower Higher
Example "running" → "runn" "running" → "run"
Example "better" → "better" "better" → "good"
Tool NLTK PorterStemmer NLTK WordNetLemmatizer, spaCy

Step 5: Feature Extraction

Convert text tokens to numerical vectors that ML models can process.

  • Bag of Words (BoW) — word frequency counts
  • TF-IDF — weighs term importance across documents
  • Word2Vec / GloVe — dense semantic embeddings
  • BERT Embeddings — context-aware representations

Topic 9: Phases / Levels of NLP 🔥 GUARANTEED 7 MARKS — ALL 4 PAPERS

NLP analysis proceeds through six hierarchical phases, from the smallest sound units up to real-world context and speaker intent.

# Phase What It Analyses Key Concepts Example
1 Phonological Sounds and phonemes of language Phoneme mapping, speech-to-text, prosody "cat" has 3 phonemes: /k/, /æ/, /t/
2 Morphological Internal structure of words Morphemes, stemming, inflection, derivation "unhappiness" = un- + happy + -ness
3 Syntactic Grammar rules & sentence structure Parsing, parse trees, POS tagging, CFG "The dog bit the man" — Subject-Verb-Object
4 Semantic Meaning of words and sentences Word sense disambiguation, semantic roles, NER "bank" = financial institution (not river bank)
5 Discourse Meaning across multiple sentences Coreference resolution, coherence, cohesion "John fell. He got hurt." — "He" refers to John
6 Pragmatic Real-world context & speaker intent Speech acts, implicature, world knowledge "Can you open the window?" = Please open it
